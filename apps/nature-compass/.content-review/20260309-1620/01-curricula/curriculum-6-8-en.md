# Curriculum: 6-8 (EN)

Theme: 城市湿地生态探索
City: 武汉
Duration: 180min
Lessons: 4

## Lesson 1: Floating Friends: Wetland Plants
- **Theme:** Wetland Flora and Buoyancy
- **Location:** Jiefang Park (解放公园), Jiang'an District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Art
- **Goals:** Identify and name 3 different wetland plants in English; Understand why some plants float while others sink; Observe and draw plant structures (leaves, stems, roots)
- **Activities:** Students will go on a 'Plant Scavenger Hunt' to find specific leaves and flowers near the park ponds. They will then conduct a simple 'Float or Sink' experiment using natural materials found on the ground to understand plant buoyancy.
- **Safety:** Keep students at least 2 meters away from the water's edge; use long-handled nets for any plant collection; watch for slippery moss.

## Lesson 2: Sky Dancers: The Bird Watchers
- **Theme:** Wetland Birds and Habitats
- **Location:** Sha Lake Park (沙湖公园), Wuchang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Math, Engineering
- **Goals:** Count birds and use numbers 1-20 in a real-world context; Identify bird body parts and basic movements (fly, swim, walk); Understand the concept of a 'habitat' as a home for animals
- **Activities:** Using child-friendly binoculars, students will participate in a 'Bird Census' to count how many white egrets and ducks they see. They will then use clay and twigs to build a model nest based on their observations of the environment.
- **Safety:** Ensure students do not feed the birds; stay on the designated wooden boardwalks; use sun protection in open areas.

## Lesson 3: Hidden Water World: Mini-Beasts
- **Theme:** Aquatic Life and Magnification
- **Location:** East Lake Wetland Park (东湖湿地公园 - 落雁景区), Hongshan District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Technology, Art
- **Goals:** Use magnifying glasses to observe small insects and fish; Use adjectives (big, small, fast, slow) to describe aquatic life; Learn the life cycle of a frog or dragonfly through observation
- **Activities:** Students will use magnifying jars to safely observe water samples and identify 'mini-beasts' like water striders or small fry. They will create a 'Water Life Map' by drawing the creatures they find and labeling their speed and size.
- **Safety:** Strict supervision near the lake shore; ensure students wash hands after touching pond water; watch for stinging insects.

## Lesson 4: Wetland Guardians: Clean Water Heroes
- **Theme:** Water Filtration and Conservation
- **Location:** Moon Lake Park (月湖公园), Hanyang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Engineering, Science, Art
- **Goals:** Understand that wetlands help clean our city's water; Follow a 3-step English instruction to build a simple filter; Express simple ways to 'help' the earth (e.g., 'Do not litter')
- **Activities:** Students will act as 'Engineers' to build a natural water filter using sand, pebbles, and charcoal in a plastic bottle. They will test how the wetland-inspired layers can turn 'cloudy' water into 'clear' water and discuss how to protect the lake.
- **Safety:** Remind students that the filtered water is for observation only and NOT for drinking; handle rocks and sand carefully to avoid eye contact.
