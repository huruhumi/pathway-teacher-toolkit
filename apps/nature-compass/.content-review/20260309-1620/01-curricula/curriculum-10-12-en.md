# Curriculum: 10-12 (EN)

Theme: 城市湿地生态探索
City: 武汉
Duration: 180min
Lessons: 4

## Lesson 1: The Secret Life of Water
- **Theme:** Water Quality and Filtration
- **Location:** Jiefang Park (解放公园)
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Engineering
- **Goals:** Identify the basic functions of a wetland in filtering city water.; Use comparative adjectives (cleaner, dirtier, clearer) to describe water samples.; Build a natural water filtration model using sand, gravel, and charcoal.
- **Activities:** Students collect water samples from the park's ponds and test their clarity. They then work in teams to build a multi-layer natural filter and record how the water changes as it passes through.
- **Safety:** Stay 2 meters away from the water edge; use long-handled cups for sampling; wash hands after touching pond water.

## Lesson 2: Feathered Friends of the Lake
- **Theme:** Bird Observation and Adaptation
- **Location:** East Lake Wetland Park (东湖湿地公园 - 落雁景区)
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Art
- **Goals:** Describe bird physical traits and behaviors using the present continuous tense.; Explain how different beak shapes help birds eat specific foods.; Use binoculars and a field guide to identify at least 3 local bird species.
- **Activities:** Students go on a guided bird walk to spot migratory birds like the Grey Heron. They complete a 'Beak and Food' matching challenge and sketch birds in their field journals.
- **Safety:** Keep noise levels low to avoid scaring wildlife; stay on the designated boardwalks; wear warm clothing.

## Lesson 3: The Green Scavenger Hunt
- **Theme:** Plant Biodiversity and Classification
- **Location:** Sha Lake Park (沙湖公园)
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Math, Art
- **Goals:** Classify aquatic plants into floating, submerged, and emergent categories.; Use sequence adverbs (first, next, then) to explain the process of photosynthesis.; Measure and record leaf dimensions to practice data collection.
- **Activities:** Students participate in a botanical scavenger hunt to find specific wetland plants. They use rulers to measure plant height and create leaf rubbings to study vein patterns.
- **Safety:** Wear sunscreen and hats; watch out for slippery stones near the lotus ponds; do not pick or eat any wild plants.

## Lesson 4: Eco-Engineers: Protecting the Shore
- **Theme:** Conservation and Urban Impact
- **Location:** Moon Lake (月湖公园)
- **STEAM Focus:** Engineering, Technology, Art
- **Goals:** Propose 3 ways to reduce human impact on urban wetlands.; Use modal verbs (should/shouldn't) to create park rules for visitors.; Design a model of a 'Trash Trap' to prevent plastic from entering the lake.
- **Activities:** Students conduct a 'Trash Audit' to see what types of waste are near the lake. They then use recycled materials to design and pitch a prototype device that protects the wetland from city pollution.
- **Safety:** Use gloves and tongs when picking up trash; avoid sharp objects like broken glass; stay within sight of the instructors.
