# Curriculum: 城市湿地生态探索 (Urban Wetland Ecology Exploration) — Age 6-8 (EN)

## Overview
This 4-lesson STEAM curriculum is designed for 6-8 year old students with A1 English proficiency in Wuhan. It aims to introduce them to the fascinating world of urban wetland ecosystems through hands-on outdoor exploration. Students will learn about wetland plants and animals, understand the importance of water quality, and explore conservation efforts. Each 90-minute lesson integrates multiple STEAM disciplines, focuses on specific ESL skills, and includes a practical indoor alternative for rainy days, all while utilizing prominent outdoor locations in Wuhan.

## Lesson 1: Lesson 1: Wetland Wonders - Plants and Their Homes
- **Description**: Students will embark on an exciting journey to discover the diverse plant life of urban wetlands. They will learn to identify common wetland plants, understand their basic structures, and appreciate their role in the ecosystem. Activities will encourage observation, sketching, and simple counting.
- **STEAM Focus**: Biology & Ecology (plant identification, ecosystem roles), Visual Arts (nature sketching), Math & Logic (counting, pattern recognition)
- **ESL Focus**: Naming objects ('This is a leaf.', 'I see a flower.'), using simple descriptive adjectives (green, tall, small), and asking/answering 'What is this?'.
- **Location**: Wuhan Botanical Garden (specifically the wetland exhibition areas)
- **Outdoor Activity**: Upon arrival, students receive a 'Wetland Plant Passport' with pictures of 5-7 common wetland plants (e.g., lotus, water lily, cattail). They work in small groups with a guide to find and identify these plants, marking them off their passport. For each plant, they observe its parts (leaf, stem, flower, root if visible) and sketch one leaf or flower. They will also collect fallen leaves for leaf rubbings. The activity concludes with a group sharing of their findings and favorite plant observations.
- **Indoor Alternative**: Students will be provided with pictures of various wetland plants and their parts. They will engage in a 'Plant Parts Matching Game' where they match labels (leaf, flower, stem, root) to the correct parts. Following this, they will draw their favorite wetland plant from memory or provided images, labeling the parts in English.
- **Vocabulary**: plant, leaf, flower, root, stem, water, green, tall, short, tree, grass, soil, find, see, this, is

## Lesson 2: Lesson 2: Water World - Aquatic Animals and Habitats
- **Description**: This lesson focuses on the fascinating aquatic animals that call wetlands home. Students will learn about different types of wetland creatures, their unique adaptations for living in water, and the importance of their habitats. Activities will involve careful observation and understanding animal behaviors.
- **STEAM Focus**: Biology & Ecology (animal identification, habitats, adaptations), Physics & Forces (how animals move in water), Engineering & Design (simple habitat understanding)
- **ESL Focus**: Describing animals using simple adjectives (big, small, fast, slow, blue, brown), using action verbs (swim, fly, jump), and asking/answering 'What animal is this?' and 'Where does it live?'.
- **Location**: East Lake National Scenic Area (near the lake's edge, designated safe observation points)
- **Outdoor Activity**: Students will participate in 'Pond Life Safari'. Using nets and observation trays (under strict supervision and safety guidelines), they will carefully collect and observe small aquatic creatures (e.g., tadpoles, small fish, water insects) from the shallow edges of the lake. After observation, all creatures will be safely returned. Students will describe what they see using English vocabulary, focusing on movement and basic features. A guide will lead discussions on how these animals move and what their 'home' (habitat) looks like. The lesson will also include observing ducks and other birds on the lake, imitating their sounds and movements.
- **Indoor Alternative**: Students will create 'Wetland Animal Puppets' using pre-cut shapes and craft materials. They will then put on a simple 'Animal Puppet Show', describing their animal's appearance and actions ('My frog jumps!', 'My duck swims in the water.') to their classmates. They will also draw a picture of their favorite wetland animal in its habitat.
- **Vocabulary**: fish, duck, bird, frog, turtle, insect, swim, fly, jump, walk, big, small, blue, brown, home, pond, lake, live, move

## Lesson 3: Lesson 3: Wetland Health - Water Quality and Conservation
- **Description**: Students will explore the critical concept of water quality in wetlands and understand why it's important to protect these environments. They will conduct simple observations to assess water health and learn about human impacts, fostering a sense of responsibility towards conservation.
- **STEAM Focus**: Chemistry & Matter (observing water properties), Earth & Space (simplified water cycle), Social Science (human impact, conservation), Biology & Ecology (impact on living things)
- **ESL Focus**: Expressing simple opinions ('I like clean water.', 'Dirty water is bad.'), using 'yes/no' questions, and understanding simple cause-and-effect ('If water is dirty, animals are sad.').
- **Location**: Shahu Park (focus on the lake and surrounding areas)
- **Outdoor Activity**: The lesson begins with a 'Water Quality Detective' activity. Students will observe water samples (safely collected by adults) from different parts of the wetland (e.g., clearer vs. murkier areas). They will use their senses (sight, smell – no tasting!) to describe the water's color, clarity, and any visible debris. They will also participate in a supervised 'Wetland Cleanup Challenge' (wearing gloves) to collect small pieces of litter from designated areas, discussing why it's important to keep the wetland clean. The activity concludes with a group discussion on 'How can we help the wetland stay healthy?'
- **Indoor Alternative**: Students will create a 'Water Cycle Craft' using paper plates, cotton balls, and blue paper to represent evaporation, condensation, and precipitation. They will also play a 'Clean vs. Dirty Water Sorting Game' where they sort pictures of items that belong in a healthy wetland versus items that pollute it. Finally, they will draw 'Healthy Wetland' posters with simple English slogans like 'Keep Water Clean!'
- **Vocabulary**: clean, dirty, water, safe, trash, help, protect, river, lake, good, bad, happy, sad, see, smell, color

## Lesson 4: Lesson 4: Designing Future Wetlands - Ecosystem Engineers
- **Description**: In the final lesson, students will synthesize their learning by engaging in a creative design challenge. They will review the components of a healthy wetland and use natural materials to design and build a miniature wetland model, applying principles of engineering and ecological understanding.
- **STEAM Focus**: Engineering & Design (planning, building models, problem-solving), Math & Logic (measuring, spatial reasoning), Biology & Ecology (reviewing ecosystem components), Visual Arts (model aesthetics, creativity)
- **ESL Focus**: Giving simple instructions ('Put this here.', 'We need more rocks.'), comparing and contrasting ('This is bigger than that.', 'Both have water.'), and expressing ideas ('My idea is...').
- **Location**: Wuhan Garden Expo Park (specifically areas featuring diverse garden designs and water features)
- **Outdoor Activity**: Students will begin with a 'Garden Design Inspiration Walk' through different sections of the Garden Expo Park, observing how water features, plants, and rocks are arranged. They will then be divided into small groups and given a base (e.g., a shallow tray or cardboard box lid). Their challenge is to 'Design Your Own Mini Wetland' using natural materials found in a designated collection area (e.g., small stones, twigs, leaves, sand, small amounts of water). They will plan their design, considering where the 'water' will go, where 'plants' will be, and how 'animals' might live there. Groups will then build their models and present them to the class, explaining their design choices in simple English.
- **Indoor Alternative**: Students will work collaboratively to build a 'Large Wetland Diorama' using various craft materials (construction paper, pipe cleaners, clay, small plastic animals). They will draw and cut out wetland elements, arrange them, and glue them into a shared diorama. Each group will then present their section of the diorama, explaining what they built and why, using the target English vocabulary and sentence structures.
- **Vocabulary**: build, design, plan, measure, big, small, more, less, together, finish, my, your, our, beautiful, idea, rock, stick, sand, model
