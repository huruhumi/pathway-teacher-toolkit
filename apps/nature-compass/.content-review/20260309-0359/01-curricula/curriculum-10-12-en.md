# Curriculum: 城市湿地生态探索 (Urban Wetland Ecological Exploration) — Age 10-12 (EN)

## Overview
This 4-lesson STEAM outdoor curriculum is designed for 10-12 year old students in Wuhan, with an A2 English level. Centered around the theme of 'Urban Wetland Ecological Exploration', it aims to immerse students in the rich biodiversity and ecological importance of Wuhan's urban wetlands. Through hands-on activities, observation, and creative expression at specific local wetland parks, students will develop a deeper understanding of biology, ecology, environmental science, and engineering principles, while simultaneously enhancing their English language skills. Each 90-minute lesson is progressive, building knowledge and fostering a sense of environmental stewardship.

## Lesson 1: Wetland Wonders: Discovering Life at Jinyinhu
- **Description**: This lesson introduces students to the concept of wetlands and their incredible biodiversity. Students will engage in a guided exploration to identify various plant and animal species, learning about their habitats and adaptations. The lesson culminates in a nature sketching activity to observe and record their findings, fostering both scientific observation and artistic expression. Through interactive discussions and a 'Wetland Scavenger Hunt', students will begin to appreciate the unique characteristics of wetland ecosystems.
- **STEAM Focus**: Biology & Ecology (identifying species, habitats, adaptations), Visual Arts (nature sketching, observation), Social Science (importance of wetlands to local communities).
- **ESL Focus**: Students will practice naming and describing wetland animals and plants using simple adjectives (e.g., 'This is a big green leaf', 'I see a small bird'). They will also use 'There is/are' to describe observations.
- **Location**: 武汉金银湖国家城市湿地公园 (Wuhan Jinyinhu National Urban Wetland Park)
- **Outdoor Activity**: 90 minutes: 1. Introduction & Safety Briefing (10 min): What is a wetland? Why is it important? Rules for exploration. 2. Wetland Scavenger Hunt (30 min): Students receive a checklist with pictures/simple descriptions of common wetland plants (e.g., lotus, reeds) and animals (e.g., ducks, dragonflies). They work in small groups to find and tick off items. 3. Habitat Observation & Discussion (20 min): Guided walk to different microhabitats (e.g., water's edge, grassy areas). Discuss what lives there and why. 4. Nature Sketching (20 min): Students choose a plant or animal they observed and sketch it in their nature journals, labeling key features with English words. 5. Share & Review (10 min): Groups share their scavenger hunt findings and sketches, practicing descriptive English.
- **Indoor Alternative**: 90 minutes: 1. Virtual Wetland Tour (20 min): Watch a short documentary or virtual tour of a wetland, focusing on Jinyinhu if possible. 2. Wetland Animal & Plant Matching Game (30 min): Students match pictures of wetland flora and fauna with their English names and simple descriptions. 3. 'Design Your Own Wetland Creature' (30 min): Students draw an imaginary wetland animal, giving it a name and describing its adaptations (e.g., 'It has long legs to walk in water'). 4. Vocabulary Review (10 min): Group discussion of new words.
- **Vocabulary**: wetland, park, lake, river, plant, tree, flower, bird, fish, insect, frog, water, green, big, small, beautiful, important, leaf, root, wing, leg, habitat, observe, sketch

## Lesson 2: The Water Story: Quality & Connections at East Lake
- **Description**: This lesson focuses on the critical role of water in wetland ecosystems and the concept of food webs. Students will conduct simple water quality tests to understand environmental indicators and observe aquatic life. They will then learn how different organisms are interconnected through food chains, culminating in the creation of a local wetland food web diagram based on their observations at East Lake.
- **STEAM Focus**: Chemistry & Matter (simple water quality testing - pH, temperature), Biology & Ecology (food chains, interdependence, aquatic life), Math & Logic (data collection, simple comparisons).
- **ESL Focus**: Students will practice explaining cause and effect relationships (e.g., 'If the water is dirty, fish cannot live here'). They will also use comparative adjectives to describe water quality (e.g., 'This water is cleaner than that water').
- **Location**: 东湖绿道 (East Lake Greenway) - 湿地段 (Wetland Section, e.g., near Moshan or Luoyan Island)
- **Outdoor Activity**: 90 minutes: 1. Introduction to Water Quality (10 min): Discuss why clean water is important for wetlands and all living things. 2. Simple Water Testing (30 min): In small groups, students use pH strips and thermometers to test water samples from different points in the wetland (e.g., near the shore, slightly deeper). Record data in a simple chart. Discuss findings (e.g., 'What is the pH?', 'Is the water warm or cold?'). 3. Aquatic Life Observation (20 min): Use magnifying glasses and small nets (catch and release) to observe small aquatic insects or plants. Discuss what they eat. 4. Wetland Food Web Activity (20 min): Based on observations, students draw or use provided cards to build a simple food web, connecting organisms with arrows. 5. Share & Conclude (10 min): Groups present their food webs and discuss the importance of clean water for all parts of the ecosystem.
- **Indoor Alternative**: 90 minutes: 1. 'Water Cycle' Story & Diagram (20 min): Review the water cycle and its importance. 2. Water Pollution Experiment (30 min): Demonstrate how different pollutants (e.g., oil, soil, 'trash') affect water in clear containers. Discuss the impact. 3. Food Web Puzzle/Game (30 min): Students complete a puzzle or play a game to build different wetland food chains and webs. 4. Vocabulary Review (10 min): Discuss new terms and their meanings.
- **Vocabulary**: clean, dirty, clear, muddy, pH, temperature, test, connect, eat, live, food chain, predator, prey, healthy, unhealthy, aquatic, insect, data, measure, result, important, pollution

## Lesson 3: Human & Wetland: Engineering Solutions at Shahu Park
- **Description**: This lesson explores the interaction between humans and urban wetlands, focusing on conservation efforts and sustainable design. Students will observe human-made structures within Shahu Park that help manage and protect the wetland. They will discuss the impact of human activities on wetlands and brainstorm engineering solutions for conservation, culminating in a design challenge to create a sustainable wetland feature.
- **STEAM Focus**: Engineering & Design (observing structures, problem-solving, designing solutions), Social Science (human impact, conservation, community roles), Earth & Space (water cycle, pollution management).
- **ESL Focus**: Students will practice expressing opinions and suggesting solutions using modal verbs (e.g., 'We should protect the wetland', 'We could build a new bridge'). They will also use 'because' to explain their reasoning.
- **Location**: 沙湖公园 (Shahu Park)
- **Outdoor Activity**: 90 minutes: 1. Introduction to Urban Wetlands & Human Impact (15 min): Discuss how cities and people interact with wetlands. What are some problems? What are some solutions? 2. Observation of Wetland Management Features (30 min): Guided walk to observe boardwalks, waste bins, plant filtering areas, or other visible engineering solutions in Shahu Park. Discuss their purpose and effectiveness. 3. 'Problem & Solution' Discussion (20 min): In small groups, identify a specific human impact observed (e.g., litter, noise) and brainstorm simple engineering or design solutions. 4. Design a Sustainable Wetland Feature (20 min): Students sketch a design for a new feature (e.g., a special bin for recycling, a new type of boardwalk, a plant filter) that helps protect or improve the wetland. 5. Share Designs (5 min): Groups briefly present their designs and explain their ideas.
- **Indoor Alternative**: 90 minutes: 1. 'Wetland Protection Heroes' Brainstorm (20 min): Discuss what individuals and communities can do to protect wetlands. 2. Design a Wetland Park Model (40 min): Using craft materials, students design and build a small model of a sustainable wetland park, incorporating features they discussed. 3. 'If I Were a Park Manager' Role-Play (20 min): Students take on the role of a park manager and explain their plans for wetland conservation. 4. Vocabulary Review (10 min): Reinforce new vocabulary.
- **Vocabulary**: protect, save, build, design, pollution, trash, clean up, human, impact, solution, idea, future, boardwalk, bridge, manage, engineer, community, recycle, reduce

## Lesson 4: My Wetland Pledge: Creative Expression & Stewardship
- **Description**: This final lesson encourages students to synthesize their learning and express their understanding of wetland importance through creative means. Revisiting Jinyinhu, students will reflect on their journey and create 'Wetland Hero' posters or short skits to advocate for wetland conservation. The lesson aims to inspire a sense of personal responsibility and future stewardship, empowering students to become advocates for these vital ecosystems.
- **STEAM Focus**: Visual Arts (poster design, creative expression), Theater & Drama (short skit, public speaking), Social Science (advocacy, community engagement, environmental ethics), English Language Arts (persuasive language, summarizing).
- **ESL Focus**: Students will practice presenting information clearly and using persuasive language to convince others of the importance of wetlands. They will also summarize their key learnings from the curriculum.
- **Location**: 武汉金银湖国家城市湿地公园 (Wuhan Jinyinhu National Urban Wetland Park) - revisit for reflection and creation
- **Outdoor Activity**: 90 minutes: 1. Recap & Reflection (15 min): Review what they learned over the past three lessons. Discuss their favorite parts and new discoveries. 'Why are wetlands important to me?' 2. 'Wetland Hero' Creative Project (50 min): In small groups, students choose to either: a) Create a 'Wetland Hero' poster: Design a poster with drawings and English slogans to encourage wetland protection. b) Prepare a short skit: Develop a 2-3 minute skit about the importance of wetlands or a solution to a wetland problem. 3. Presentation & Pledge (20 min): Groups present their posters or perform their skits to the class. Each student makes a personal 'Wetland Pledge' (e.g., 'I will not throw trash in the park'). 4. Farewell & Future Action (5 min): Discuss how they can continue to be wetland heroes.
- **Indoor Alternative**: 90 minutes: 1. 'My Wetland Story' Writing (20 min): Students write a short paragraph or poem about their experiences and what they learned. 2. Digital Presentation Creation (40 min): Using tablets or computers, students create simple digital slides (with pictures and text) to present their 'Wetland Hero' message. 3. Group Discussion & Debate (20 min): Discuss different ways to spread awareness about wetland protection. 4. Vocabulary Review & Curriculum Wrap-up (10 min): Final review of key vocabulary and concepts.
- **Vocabulary**: share, learn, protect, help, future, important, beautiful, amazing, create, present, message, thank you, hero, pledge, advocate, conserve, ecosystem, responsibility, proud
