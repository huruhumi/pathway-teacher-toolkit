# Curriculum: 10-12 (EN)

Theme: 城市湿地生态探索
City: 武汉
Duration: 180min
Lessons: 4

## Lesson 1: The Secret Life of Reeds
- **Theme:** Wetland Biodiversity and Ecosystems
- **Location:** Jiefang Park (解放公园) - Artificial Wetland Area
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Arts
- **Goals:** Identify and name 5 common wetland plants and animals in English.; Understand the definition of an 'ecosystem' through direct observation.; Practice using descriptive adjectives to record nature findings in a field journal.
- **Activities:** Students will go on a nature scavenger hunt to find specific wetland species and create a 'Habitat Map' of the park's pond area. They will use magnifying glasses to look at plant structures and present one 'discovery' to the group.
- **Safety:** Stay behind the wooden railings near the water; watch for slippery moss on stones.

## Lesson 2: Clear Water Quest
- **Theme:** Water Quality and Natural Filtration
- **Location:** East Lake (东湖) - Tingtao Scenic Area
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Engineering, Math
- **Goals:** Explain how wetlands act as natural water filters using simple sequence connectors (first, then, finally).; Measure water transparency using a basic Secchi disk tool.; Compare 'muddy' vs 'filtered' water samples using comparative adjectives.
- **Activities:** After testing the clarity of East Lake's water, students will work in teams to build a DIY water filtration tower using sand, gravel, and charcoal. They will predict which materials clean the water most effectively.
- **Safety:** Wear waterproof shoes; do not touch or drink the lake water samples.

## Lesson 3: Feathered Friends of Wuhan
- **Theme:** Ornithology and Bird Migration
- **Location:** Moon Lake (月湖公园)
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Technology
- **Goals:** Identify 3 local bird species and describe their physical features (beaks, feathers, wings).; Understand the concept of migration and why birds choose Wuhan wetlands as a stopover.; Use binoculars correctly and record data in a bird-watching log.
- **Activities:** Students will use binoculars to observe birds around Moon Lake and the nearby Hanyang cultural area. They will participate in a 'Beak Challenge' game to learn how different beak shapes are adapted for eating different foods.
- **Safety:** Keep a safe distance from wild birds to avoid disturbing them; stay on designated walking paths.

## Lesson 4: Future Wetland Architects
- **Theme:** Urban Conservation and Sustainable Design
- **Location:** Sha Lake Park (沙湖公园)
- **STEAM Focus:** Engineering, Arts, Science
- **Goals:** Identify human-made threats to wetlands like plastic waste and urban noise.; Propose a creative solution to protect the wetland using 'should' and 'must' for suggestions.; Design a 3D model of a 'Sustainable Wetland Park' using recycled and natural materials.
- **Activities:** Students will conduct a mini 'Trash Audit' to see how humans affect Sha Lake. They will then work in groups to sketch and build a model of a wetland that includes features for both people and animals to coexist safely.
- **Safety:** Use gloves when picking up any trash for the audit; be careful with scissors and glue during the modeling phase.
