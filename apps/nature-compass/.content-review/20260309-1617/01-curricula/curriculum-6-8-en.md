# Curriculum: 6-8 (EN)

Theme: 城市湿地生态探索
City: 武汉
Duration: 180min
Lessons: 4

## Lesson 1: Wetland Detectives: The Green Sponge
- **Theme:** Introduction to Wetland Ecosystems
- **Location:** Jiefang Park (Wetland Garden), Jiang'an District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Art
- **Goals:** Identify and name 3 basic parts of a wetland plant.; Understand that wetlands absorb water like a sponge.; Use 'I see...' and 'It is...' to describe colors and textures.
- **Activities:** Students will go on a sensory scavenger hunt to touch mud and leaves, then perform a 'Sponge Experiment' to see how wetlands prevent flooding by absorbing water. They will end by sketching their favorite plant in a field journal.
- **Safety:** Stay on the wooden boardwalks; do not touch unknown berries or flowers.

## Lesson 2: Feathered Friends of East Lake
- **Theme:** Bird Observation and Adaptation
- **Location:** East Lake Greenway (Liyuan Entrance), Wuchang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Technology, Mathematics
- **Goals:** Use binoculars to spot and count 3 different types of birds.; Compare different bird beaks and explain what they eat.; Practice using action verbs like 'fly', 'swim', and 'eat'.
- **Activities:** Students will use binoculars to observe migratory birds on East Lake and use a 'Beak Tool Kit' (tweezers, spoons, clips) to simulate how different birds catch food in the water. They will record their findings in a tally chart.
- **Safety:** Keep a safe distance from the water edge; watch out for bicycles on the Greenway.

## Lesson 3: Mini-Beasts in the Mud
- **Theme:** Insects and Biodiversity
- **Location:** Hankou Jiangtan (Phase 3 Marshlands), Jiang'an District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Engineering
- **Goals:** Find and describe insects living in the tall grass and mud.; Count the number of legs and body parts on a bug.; Use prepositions 'on', 'under', and 'in' to describe bug locations.
- **Activities:** Using magnifying glasses, students will explore the riverbank marsh to find 'mini-beasts'. They will build a temporary 'Bug Hotel' using sticks and leaves to understand insect habitats before releasing the bugs.
- **Safety:** Wear long pants to avoid mosquito bites; stay away from the deep river current.

## Lesson 4: Nature's Water Filter
- **Theme:** Water Quality and Conservation
- **Location:** Moon Lake (Yuehu) Park, Hanyang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Engineering, Science, Art
- **Goals:** Demonstrate how sand and stones can make dirty water look clear.; Understand why humans must protect clean water for animals.; Use 'First', 'Next', and 'Last' to describe a process.
- **Activities:** Students will collect lake water and build a natural filtration tower using layers of pebbles, sand, and charcoal. They will observe the change in water clarity and discuss how to keep Wuhan's lakes clean.
- **Safety:** Do not drink the lake water or the filtered water; wash hands thoroughly after the activity.
