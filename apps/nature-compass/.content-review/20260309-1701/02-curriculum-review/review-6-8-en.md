## Top 5 Specific Improvements

1.  **Develop Comprehensive Indoor Alternatives for Each Lesson:** Create detailed indoor lesson plans for each outdoor activity, ensuring they maintain the core STEAM and ESL learning objectives. These alternatives are crucial for safety and continuity during adverse weather conditions (e.g., heavy rain, extreme heat/cold, high air pollution, or unexpected park closures in Wuhan). For example, a "virtual bird watching" using multimedia, plant pressing/rubbing activities, water quality simulation with household items, or building a wetland diorama.

2.  **Clarify and Structure Lesson Duration with Pacing:** Explicitly state whether "180min" refers to the total curriculum or each lesson. If it's 180 minutes per lesson, it is too long; break it down into shorter, distinct segments (e.g., 30-45 minute activity blocks with built-in breaks and transitions) to match the attention span of 6-8 year olds. If it's 180 minutes total (45 min/lesson), provide a suggested minute-by-minute breakdown for each lesson to ensure all activities are feasible within the timeframe, including setup, instruction, activity time, and wrap-up.

3.  **Enhance ESL Scaffolding and Interactive Speaking Opportunities:** For CEFR A1 students, explicitly integrate more language support. This includes:
    *   **Pre-teaching:** Introduce new vocabulary (e.g., "macro-invertebrates," "repel," "conservation") with flashcards, realia, and simple definitions before each lesson.
    *   **Interactive Speaking:** Incorporate simple Q&A sessions, "turn and talk" activities (e.g., "Tell your partner what you found"), or short, repetitive chants/songs related to the theme to encourage active language use.
    *   **Sentence Frames:** Provide additional sentence frames beyond the stated goals (e.g., "The plant feels [adjective]," "I think this water is [clean/dirty] because...").

4.  **Integrate Technology More Explicitly and Hands-On:** While "Technology" is listed as a STEAM focus, its application is often implicit or absent.
    *   **Lesson 2 (Aquatic Plants):** Beyond the "Lotus Effect" experiment (which is science), consider using simple digital microscopes connected to a tablet to observe plant structures, or a plant identification app.
    *   **Lesson 3 (Macro-invertebrates):** Introduce a simple data collection app on a tablet for students to record their counts, or use a digital camera to document findings before release. This makes the "Technology" aspect more tangible for young learners.

5.  **Incorporate Sensory and Movement Breaks:** For young children, especially in an outdoor setting, regular short breaks are essential for managing energy and attention. Suggest specific points within each lesson for a quick stretch, a sensory exploration unrelated to the main task (e.g., listening to ambient sounds, feeling the wind), or a brief game to re-energize and refocus attention, particularly if lessons are longer than 45 minutes.
