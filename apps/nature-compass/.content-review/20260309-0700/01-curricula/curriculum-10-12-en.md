# Curriculum: 10-12 (EN)

Theme: 城市湿地生态探索
City: 武汉
Duration: 180min
Lessons: 4

## Lesson 1: Wetland Detectives: Finding the City's Lungs
- **Theme:** Introduction to Wetland Ecosystems
- **Location:** Jiefang Park (解放公园), Jiang'an District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Art, Mathematics
- **Goals:** Identify the three main parts of a wetland: water, soil, and plants.; Use 'There is/There are' to describe nature observations in English.; Create a basic map of the park's wetland area using symbols.
- **Activities:** Students will go on a scavenger hunt to find different wetland features and take photos of local plants. They will then work in teams to draw a 'Nature Map' showing how water moves through the park.
- **Safety:** Stay on the designated wooden boardwalks; do not touch unknown berries or fungi; keep a safe distance from the water edge.

## Lesson 2: The Great Water Filter Challenge
- **Theme:** Water Quality and Natural Filtration
- **Location:** East Lake - Tingtao Scenic Area (东湖听涛景区)
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Engineering, Mathematics
- **Goals:** Measure water transparency using a DIY Secchi disk.; Explain the process of natural filtration using sequence adverbs (First, Then, Finally).; Compare water samples from different locations in the lake.
- **Activities:** Students will collect water samples and test their clarity. They will build a small-scale model of a natural filter using sand, gravel, and charcoal to see how wetlands clean our water.
- **Safety:** Wear waterproof gloves when handling lake water; use the 'buddy system' near the docks; do not drink the lake water.

## Lesson 3: Nature's Superpowers: Plant Adaptations
- **Theme:** Botany and Biomimicry
- **Location:** Moon Lake Park (月湖公园), Hanyang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Technology, Art
- **Goals:** Describe how lotus leaves stay dry using the 'Lotus Effect'.; Use comparative adjectives (bigger, smoother, greener) to describe wetland plants.; Design a waterproof product inspired by plant structures.
- **Activities:** Students will use magnifying glasses to inspect lotus leaves and aquatic plants. They will perform a 'water drop test' on different surfaces and sketch an invention based on plant adaptations.
- **Safety:** Be mindful of high temperatures and stay in the shade; apply mosquito repellent; avoid leaning over the stone railings.

## Lesson 4: Winged Neighbors: Bird Watching and Food Chains
- **Theme:** Ornithology and Biodiversity
- **Location:** Sha Lake Park (沙湖公园), Wuchang District
- **STEAM Focus:** Science, Mathematics, Technology
- **Goals:** Identify three common wetland birds using binoculars.; Build a simple food chain model showing energy flow from plants to birds.; Practice using action verbs to describe bird behaviors (flying, diving, nesting).
- **Activities:** Using binoculars, students will conduct a bird census and record their findings in a field journal. They will then participate in a role-play game to understand how energy moves through the wetland food web.
- **Safety:** Use binocular neck straps at all times; keep noise levels low to avoid disturbing wildlife; watch for slippery leaves on the paths.
